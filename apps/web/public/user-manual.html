Introduction
What is AMIS and how it works
Section 1
All Users

AMIS is a multi-tenant Academic Management Information System designed for technical and vocational education institutions. It manages the full student lifecycle — from public admission applications through enrolment, marks, fees, clearance, and alumni tracking — while supporting multi-institute deployments from a single platform.

Key Concepts

TermMeaning
TenantA single institution (e.g., Kasese Technical Institute)
Academic YearA calendar year of study (e.g., 2025/2026)
TermA study period within an academic year (Term 1, 2, 3)
ProgrammeA course of study (e.g., Diploma in ICT)
IntakeThe year a student cohort was admitted (e.g., 2024)
SubmissionA batch of marks submitted by an instructor

Getting Started — Login & Navigation
How to sign in, reset your password, and navigate the system
Section 2
All Users

Logging In

  1. Open your browser and navigate to your institution's AMIS URL.
  2. Enter your Organisation slug (e.g., kti), email address, and password.
  3. Click Sign In.
💡Forgot your password? Click Forgot password? below the sign-in button. Enter your email address — you will receive a reset link within a few minutes.

Resetting a Password

  1. Click the link in the password-reset email.
  2. Enter and confirm your new password.
  3. Click Reset Password — you will be redirected to login.

Main Navigation

After login, the left-hand sidebar lists all modules available to your role. The top header shows the institute logo and name, your current role badge, and a logout link. The current page is highlighted with a coloured left border.

Students
Managing student records, imports, and promotions
Section 4
Admin Registrar Dean HOD Instructor Finance Principal

Viewing the Student List

Navigate to Students in the sidebar. The list shows all active students by default.

Use the filter bar to:

  • Search by name or admission number
  • Filter by Academic Year — select a year of study
  • Filter by Programme — narrow to a specific programme
  • Show Inactive — toggle to include deactivated student records

Creating a Student

  1. Click + New Student.
  2. Fill in First Name, Last Name, Admission Number (unique), Programme, Intake, Date of Birth, Gender, Nationality.
  3. Optional: contact details, guardian info, district.
  4. Click Save.

Student Detail Page

The detail page shows all information across tabs: Profile, Fees, Marks, Term Registrations, Documents, and Clearance.

Importing Students (Bulk)

  1. Click Import Students (top right of the student list).
  2. Download the CSV template.
  3. Fill in the template — each row is one student.
  4. Upload the completed CSV file.
  5. Review the preview table and click Import.
ℹ️Rows with errors (e.g., duplicate admission numbers) are flagged; valid rows are still imported.

Student Promotion

  1. Click Promote Students from the student list.
  2. Select the source academic year/term and the target year.
  3. Select students to promote (or select all).
  4. Click Promote — each student's year of study is incremented.

Admission Workflow

DRAFT → SUBMITTED → UNDER_REVIEW → COMMITTEE_REVIEW → APPROVED_GOVT (government-sponsored) → APPROVED_PRIVATE (self-sponsored) → ENROLLED At any review stage → REJECTED

Creating an Application

  1. Click + New Application.
  2. Enter applicant details: name, programme, intake, email, phone.
  3. Add academic qualifications.
  4. Click Save as Draft.
✅Applications also arrive automatically via the Public Portal at /apply/:slug.

Term Registrations
The 12-step workflow to officially enrol a student for a term
Section 6
Admin Registrar Dean

Registration Workflow (12 Steps)

1. REGISTRATION_STARTED 2. → DOCUMENTS_VERIFIED 3. → FEES_VERIFIED 4. → GUILD_FEES_VERIFIED 5. → DEAN_ENDORSED 6. → HALL_ALLOCATED 7. → CATERING_VERIFIED 8. → MEDICAL_CHECKED 9. → LIBRARY_CARD_ISSUED 10. → ONLINE_REGISTERED 11. → EXAM_ENROLLED 12. → CLEARANCE_ISSUED

Creating a Registration

  1. Navigate to Term Registrations → + New Registration.
  2. Select the student (type to search), academic year, term, and programme.
  3. Click Save — created at REGISTRATION_STARTED.

Advancing a Registration

Open a record and click the appropriate transition button (e.g., Verify Documents, Verify Fees, Dean Endorse). Each step may be restricted to certain roles.

Bulk Registration

  1. Navigate to Term Registrations → Bulk Register.
  2. Select the term and programme.
  3. Choose students from the list (checkbox or select all).
  4. Click Register All — creates REGISTRATION_STARTED records for all selected students at once.

Finance & Fees
Payment recording, fee ledger, overview, and reconciliation
Section 7
Admin Finance Principal

Per-Student Fee Ledger

  1. Navigate to Finance.
  2. Search for a student by name or admission number.
  3. Summary cards show Total Due, Total Paid, Balance, and Status (PAID / PARTIAL / OWING).
  4. The transaction table below lists every payment with date, amount, method, and reference.

Recording a Payment

  1. Click Record Payment or navigate to Finance → Fee Entry.
  2. Select the student and enter: Amount, Payment method, Reference number, Payment date.
  3. Click Save.

Fee Overview (Institution-Wide)

Navigate to Finance → Overview. Summary cards show total expected revenue, total collected, collection rate (%), fully paid count, and a Defaulters Table listing students with outstanding balances.

SchoolPay Reconciliation

  1. Navigate to Finance → Reconciliation.
  2. Find an unmatched SchoolPay transaction and click Match.
  3. Search for the student and click Confirm Match.

Importing Payments (Bulk)

  1. Navigate to Finance → Import.
  2. Download the CSV template, fill in payments, and upload.
  3. Review and confirm.
🖨️Navigate to Finance → Receipt to print or preview a fee receipt for a student and term.

Results & Transcripts
Processing end-of-term results, slips, and full transcripts
Section 9
Admin Registrar Principal Dean

Processing End-of-Term Results

  1. Navigate to Results and select the term.
  2. Click Process Results — the system calculates GPA based on published marks and configured grading scales.
  3. The table shows each student's GPA and pass/fail status.
⚠️Marks must be in PUBLISHED state before running results processing.

Results Slip

  1. Navigate to Results → Result Slip.
  2. Select a student and term.
  3. Click Preview to view the formatted results slip, then Print or save as PDF.

Transcript

Navigate to Results → Transcript and select a student to generate a full academic transcript covering all completed terms.

Attendance
Recording and reviewing student attendance per course
Section 10
Instructor HOD Admin Registrar

Taking Attendance

  1. Navigate to Attendance and select: Programme, Academic Year, Term, Course, Date.
  2. Click Load Sheet.
  3. For each student, set: P Present, A Absent, L Late, E Excused.
  4. Add optional per-student notes.
  5. Click Save Attendance.

Attendance Summary

Switch to the Summary tab to view attendance rates per student across a date range, showing percentage attendance and total absences.

Timetable
Weekly class schedule grid — view, add, and edit slots
Section 11
Admin Registrar HOD Instructor (view)

Viewing the Timetable

  1. Navigate to Timetable.
  2. Select the academic year, programme, and optionally filter by term.
  3. The timetable renders as a weekly grid (Mon–Fri) with colour-coded slots per course.
  4. Each slot shows: course name, room, instructor, and time.

Adding / Editing Slots

  1. Click + Add Slot or click an empty cell.
  2. Fill in: Day, Start/End time, Course, Room, Instructor, Notes.
  3. Click Save. To edit, click a slot → modify → Update. To remove, click Delete.

Admin Studio
System configuration — all 14 sections
Section 21
Admin Only
⚠️Configuration changes only take effect after they are Published. Changes saved as drafts are not live.

Navigate to Admin Studio via the gear icon in the top navigation, or directly at /admin-studio.

Config Dashboard

Landing page showing current published config version, pending draft status, and quick-access buttons to all sections. Use Validate → Publish to go live, or Rollback to revert.

Institute Profile

Set institution name, short name, logo URL, address, contact email/phone, and website.

Branding & Theme

Customise primary colour (hex code), secondary colour, logo URL, and font family. Changes preview live before publishing.

Module Toggles

Enable or disable system modules. Disabled modules are hidden from all users' navigation.

Navigation Editor

Customise the sidebar navigation per role. Drag to reorder, add custom links, set icons, and configure visibility per role (use Role Tabs to switch between roles).

Academic Calendar

Create academic years and terms. For each year, click + Add Term and enter term number, name, start date, end date. Use Mark as Current to set the active year/term used system-wide.

Workflow Editor

View and configure the state machines for Admissions, Term Registrations, and Mark Submissions. Each workflow shows States, Transitions, and which Role can trigger each transition.

Student Form Editor

Control which fields appear on the student form — toggle on/off, mark required, reorder, add custom fields.

Admission Form Editor

Configure the public application form — visible fields, required fields, and programme-specific questions.

Fee Structure Editor

Define the official fee schedule per programme, academic year, and term.

  1. Click + Add Fee Structure.
  2. Select: Academic Year, Term, Programme, Fee Type (Tuition / Functional / Examination / Other).
  3. Select Student Category: All Students, Boarding, or Day Scholar.
  4. Enter Amount and optional description.
  5. Click Create.

Select which metric cards each role sees on the Dashboard. Use role tabs to switch, check/uncheck widgets, then Save Draft → Publish.

Publishing Configuration

  1. Make changes across any configuration sections.
  2. Return to the Config Dashboard.
  3. Click Validate — checks all schemas for errors.
  4. Click Publish — new config goes live immediately.
  5. If needed, click Rollback to revert to the previously published version.

Public Application Portal
How prospective students apply online — no login required
Section 23
Public — No Login Required

Prospective students can apply online at:

https://[your-domain]/apply/[institution-slug] e.g. https://amis.example.com/apply/kti

How to Apply

  1. Open the application URL in any browser.
  2. Fill in: First Name, Last Name, Programme (dropdown), Intake year, Email (optional), Phone (optional).
  3. Click Submit Application.
  4. Note the Reference Number shown on the success screen for follow-up.
ℹ️Submitted applications appear in Admissions in DRAFT state, ready for staff review.

Troubleshooting
Common issues and how to resolve them
Section 25
All Users
ProblemCauseSolution
"Failed to load" / blank list Network or server issue Check internet connection. Refresh the page (Ctrl+R). Contact admin if persistent.
422 Unprocessable Entity Invalid form field value Check all required fields are filled. Ensure numbers are in valid ranges. Dates must be YYYY-MM-DD.
Login fails "Invalid credentials" Wrong org slug, email, or password Verify the org slug. Use Forgot Password to reset. Contact admin if account is deactivated.
Config changes not taking effect Draft not published Go to Admin Studio → Config Dashboard and click Publish.
Marks not in Results Marks not yet PUBLISHED Navigate to Marks, find the submission. Admin/Registrar must click Publish.
Fee balance shows incorrectly Missing fee structure or wrong term Check Fee Structure is set up in Admin Studio. Ensure payments are recorded for the correct term.
Module not in sidebar Role restriction or module disabled Contact Admin to check role permissions or re-enable the module in Admin Studio → Modules.
📞For technical support, contact your system administrator or the AMIS support team.
